How Topics Are Defined

Note: The graphs below can take a few seconds to load.

When we're using non-negative matrix factorization (NMF), we can think of a "topic" as a group of words that tend to be used together in documents, or as types of documents which tend to use similar words.

After running the NMF model, topics have to be named manually based on the words that are most indicative of that topic. How much a topic is defined by a word is called that word's "topic loading". Documents also have "loadings", which show how much that document is defined by a topic.

These topic names are what I came up with based on the words that loaded most heavily on that topic. You may come up with different names, based on your field and depth of experience in machine learning.
